First off, throughout all of this guide, I will be referring to the game as LADX, short for Link’s Awakening DX. I’ll also be referring to dungeons as D1, D2, D3, etc.

Whenever the word ‘hold’ is used, you’ll want to hold down the inputs mentioned. Whenever the word ‘buffer’ is used, you’ll want to hold the input mentioned when your currently opened menu is closing, or when you’re in a transition. S&Q means save and quit. It means saving and quitting and then going back into your save file.

Getting started

To start running LADX, you’ll need an emulator and a ROM, unless you’re running on console. There’s an emulator pack provided here. Japanese 1.0 is the fastest version. Japanese text is faster and versions 1.1 and 1.2 don’t allow you to execute most of the glitches used in the run. Which of the three provided emulators you use is up to personal preference. For running on console, using a Gameboy Player or Gameboy Interface for Gamecube is the best way to be able to record your gameplay.

Some notes

Timing starts when you select a save file in the title screen and start the game. Timing ends when the screen after walking up the stairs after the Dethyl fight completely turns white. If you get a death that would save time, you have to walk back to the place you died, and then you can progress again.

Movement

Whenever you move in this game, try to minimize your diagonal movement. When you move in a straight line, you move at 16 subpixels per frame. When you move diagonally, you move at 12 subpixels per frame. This means moving diagonally is slow if you can move in a straight line. Just move diagonally if you actually need to go in a diagonal direction though. It’s faster than moving up then right for example. Jumping diagonally however, is actually the fastest form of movement in the game. Whenever you have feather equipped and you need to move diagonally, jump. Also try to avoid moving through grass and water. Jump over it if you can. Pegasus boots are another form of fast movement. It’s slower than diagonal jumps, but it’s faster than moving normally. Try to dash whenever you can, and equip boots whenever dashing would save more than 2 seconds.

Menuing

Menuing in LADX is very fast and optimized. The interesting thing about it is that your items switch places all the time. If you want optimized menuing, you’re gonna need a menu route. Most (if not all) top runners have one, and it saves actual minutes. When you’re starting out, it’s okay if you have to react to your menus, but having a menu route in your muscle memory is much faster.

Global framerule

LADX has a global framerule. This means certain events can only happen every other frame. Things like picking up instruments, falling in pits, and picking up keys. The global framerule can really affect your time and there’s nothing you can do about it.

Name selection

The amount of characters your file name has doesn’t matter. The game always uses the full 5 characters and fills up unused characters with empty space.

Chest text skips

In LADX, whenever you open a chest, you can skip the text it gives you by opening your S&Q menu. You can do this with every chest in the game. You open your S&Q menu by pressing A, B, Start and Select at the same time. Don’t open your S&Q menu too early after opening the chest though, because it won’t skip the text if you’re too early. If you’re too late the text will also still show up. So just open the chest, wait a little, and open your S&Q menu. Close your S&Q menu again as fast as possible and move on.

Moblin skip

This can be a kind of difficult skip to learn, but once you get the hang of it, it’s not that bad.

  1. The moblin needs to be facing up in front of the pit before you can do anything. You can bump him in front of it using your shield if he moves up to the right of it. If you don’t think you’ll be able to get him to face up in front of the pit, you can transition right and re-enter the screen.
  2. Once he’s in the right position, make sure you’re touching the top wall and keep bumping into him with your shield. This will move him down slightly with every bump. Keep moving him down until the bottom of his bigger arm is below the bush (left arm in the image).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           When his arm is below the bush, he’ll start moving down in bigger steps. This is another visual cue you can use. You can hold up + right to get slowed down by the wall so you have more time to react to the moblin being in position.
  3. When the moblin is far enough down, move above him. Move into the pit, but make sure you’re clipped into the wall on the right. When you move into the pit, hold up for a bit to move into it and not get stuck on the corner, and start holding up + right as soon as possible. When you’re hovering above the pit, the moblin should shoot an arrow at you. This arrow will boost you over the pit.

If you’re at half a heart after getting hit a couple times, and you haven’t gotten the skip yet, just walk into the moblin and take the death. You wouldn’t be able to survive the skip either way. You’ll be back at the exit of log cave, only one screen away. Not a very big deal. Also make sure you don’t re-enter the screen every time the moblin move the wrong way, as it’s very time consuming.

Owl skip

Transition right after moblin skip. walk towards the chest, and cut the two bushes that are in the way. This is quite a tricky skip to perform, and even harder to get consistently. Make sure you’re not wall clipped into the chest, or the skip won’t work. How to do the skip is as follows:

  1. Open the chest with either A or B.
  2. press Start the frame after you open the chest, so you see the chest open and your item menu opens as well.
  3. Close your item menu, and buffer your S&Q menu out of that.
  4. Close your S&Q menu, buffer down out of it, and keep holding down. Walk down in a straight line. Make sure not to go diagonally at all, because you won’t be able to get the skip.

You’ll see the owl land. Keep holding down until you leave the screen. You can move a little after the owl lands and walking down then should make you leave the screen. If you don’t leave the screen, you did the skip wrong. Just mash through the owl’s text and transition down afterwards if you didn’t get the skip. This chest gives you the tail key, which is used to open D1.

Trendy game

The goal here is to get the powder. Walk over to the right and interact with the shopkeeper. Select the first option using A and mash away his other text box using B. Using B is faster because it skips a text box. Walk over to the green tile and hold B until the shadow of the claw is covering the top left corner.

Tap A when the top of the powder is lined up with the arrows pointing left.

There’s a faster but riskier way of getting the powder as well. You need to be as fast as possible when interacting with the shopkeeper and moving to the green tile for this though. For the faster method, when you move the claw to the right, move it about halfway onto the arrows pointing left, and tap A immediately. This is very risky and not recommended for new runners.

While you wait for the claw to do its thing, move to the top left, clip into the wall, and stand in this vertical position. This is the furthest down you can stand, while still being guaranteed to pick up the powder on the first bounce. You can line up Link’s hat with the little square below the B button for a visual cue.

Shoplifting

When you leave the trendy game, cut the bush to your left and the one above that. The fox might  get in your way and hit you for half a heart of damage, which is why you were recommended to pick up a heart if you were low on HP before. Transition up twice and enter the shop.

Stealing from the shopkeeper can be really finicky. To steal from him, you need to do the following:

  1. Take an item from the wall.
  2. Stand above the shopkeeper with the item.
  3. Wait for the shopkeeper to turn around.
  4. After he turns around, wait about as long as it took him to turn around. There are some exceptions though. If he turns around instantly, wait ~0.5s-1s, and if it takes him very long to turn around, wait ~2-3s.
  5. After you wait, you can leave the shop. Make sure to optimize your movement here, or you might fail to steal the item. If you do fail, just move back above him and try again.

First of all, we’re going to steal the shovel. After you do this, re-enter the shop. The shopkeeper will kill you. Close the death screen and enter the shop again.

We’re going to buy some bombs now, 20 to be exact. You can buy 30 if you’re afraid you’ll waste a lot of them and run out later. This is recommended for a learning save file. There is a text skip for buying the bombs.

  1. pick up the bombs using B, and keep holding B.
  2. move to the shopkeeper.
  3. Hold Select
  4. Hold Start
  5. Hold A
  6. Your S&Q menu should show up. Close this and you’ll have bought the bombs.

After buying bombs once, re-enter the shop to get more. After you get your last set of bombs, steak the bow, and leave the shop.

Piece of power

From this point onwards, you’ll want to pay attention to how many things you kill. In LADX every kill counts towards a piece of power (PoP). A piece of power drops every 30 kills (35 after D5). It increases your movement speed by 25% and doubles your damage. You lose POP if you take 3 hits of damage to enemies. You also lose it if you change submaps (entering or leaving a cave or dungeon), if you S&Q, or if you die. Your kill count also resets if you die or S&Q. You want it to drop in dungeons 2, 3, 5, and 6. In this category, piece of power drops have been very carefully routed out. Make sure not to kill anything you don’t need to from here on out.

Super jumps

Super jumps are jumps in mid-air. You generally have be clipped into a wall to do them. You also can’t do them if you’re too close to an outwards pointing corner. There are some exceptions to needing to be clipped, but they are irrelevant for now. You can do super jumps using sword, bow, firerod, or without anything equipped. Super jumps without an item are referred to as naked super jumps.

  1. Clip into the wall
  2. Face the wall
  3. Keep pushing into the wall
  4. Jump
  5. Stop pushing into the wall and face away from it  (hold the input used to face away from it)
  6. Use sword/bow/firerod
  7. Super jump!

For naked super jumps it’s slightly different. You have to be facing away from the wall at all times. You can move left/right and keep facing up/down buy jumping and moving. So you would jump, move in mid-air, and stop moving before you land. What you can also do is use 2 directional inputs. Let’s say you’re super jumping on a south wall. To get to the right place, you can jump, start holding down + left, let go of down, then left, and you’ll be facing up in the right place. This is also how to transition while facing away from the wall: jump, start holding down + left, and let go during the transition if you don’t have to move anymore afterwards.

For naked super jumps:

  1. Clip into the wall
  2. Jump into position
  3. Jump
  4. Immediately hold down for ~0.5s after jumping
  5. Start holding the direction away from the wall
  6. Super jump!

Moldorm boss fight

If you get hit into the pit, you’ll fall down. Buffer feather + up out of that transition and you should jump straight back up. Jump over the traps again and re-enter the room. If you’ve already equipped sword, you can use map instead of item menu to cancel the text. It’s slightly faster. Just hold Select until the map closes again. spin slashes will deal 2 damage, whereas regular sword slashes will deal 1 damage. He has 4 health total.  When you enter the room for the first time, buffer sword + down out of the menu so you’re facing down with your sword out. Moldorm is very RNG based, so you have to react to what he does and act accordingly.  If you forgot  to do that, transition right and take the ladder up. If he comes straight for you, try to jump over him and release your spin slash. This spin slash will usually miss. If he moves a different direction, try to jump and release your spin slash to hit his tail. If it misses, that’s okay. After doing the spin slash, do jumps and regular sword slashes and try to hit his tail. After hitting his tail, move to his head and start spamming again.

Acorns

Acorns drop every time you kill 12 things without taking damage. If one drops, avoid it all cost. If an acorn is in effect, piece of power will not drop. Kills will still count towards piece of power, but it won’t drop. If you pick up an acorn just before a cave, for example moblin cave, just keep it and move to the cave. Caves have a different submap from the overworld. If you change submaps while you have piece of power or acorn, you’ll lose its effect.  If you’re not about to enter a cave, take 3 hits of damage to enemies and the acorn will wear off.

If you’re still low on kills, start charging your sword vertically. Transition right. Kill one or both of these moblins if you’re low on kills. Clip into the tree on the top of the screen and transition right. This is the infamous heart piece of shame. Try to avoid picking it up. Jump over the pit and then do a diagonal jump to the top right. When you’re about to land on the pit, let go of right and keep holding up. Bomb triggers

You can put down 2 bombs on a screen transition, or quickly drop 2 in a pit, and any available cutscene will get activated. This is utilized a lot throughout the run. It allows us to completely skip the ocarina for example. There are different ways to do them for different situations.

  • Vertical transitions: Press bombs + directional input into the transition on the same frame. You can buffer this type of bomb trigger by standing against the screen transition, opening your menu, and buffering the inputs out of that.
  • Horizontal transitions: Face up/down, press bombs + up/down + directional input into the transition on the same frame. If you see Link put out his hand, you have to buffer menu out of the transition and buffer bombs out of that. If you don’t see him put out his hand, the bomb trigger will work without an extra menu. You can buffer this type of bomb trigger by standing against the screen transition, opening your menu, and buffering the inputs out of that.
  • Pits: Drop 2 bombs in the pit in quick succession. You have to drop the second bomb between 3 and 5 frames after dropping the first. Mashing will usually work for this, but be careful not to waste all your bombs. You can buffer this type of bomb trigger by dropping a bomb, opening your menu, and buffering another bomb out of that. Make sure the bomb sprite is gone before you open your menu; it won’t work if the bomb sprite is still there.

Movement

Whenever you move in this game, try to minimize your diagonal movement. When you move in a straight line, you move at 16 subpixels per frame. When you move diagonally, you move at 12 subpixels per frame. This means moving diagonally is slow if you can move in a straight line. Just move diagonally if you actually need to go in a diagonal direction though. It’s faster than moving up then right for example. Jumping diagonally however, is actually the fastest form of movement in the game. Whenever you have feather equipped and you need to move diagonally, jump. Also try to avoid moving through grass and water. Jump over it if you can. Pegasus boots are another form of fast movement. It’s slower than diagonal jumps, but it’s faster than moving normally. Try to dash whenever you can, and equip boots whenever dashing would save more than 2 seconds.

Menuing

Menuing in LADX is very fast and optimized. The interesting thing about it is that your items switch places all the time. If you want optimized menuing, you’re gonna need a menu route. Most (if not all) top runners have one, and it saves actual minutes. When you’re starting out, it’s okay if you have to react to your menus, but having a menu route in your muscle memory is much faster.

Global framerule

LADX has a global framerule. This means certain events can only happen every other frame. Things like picking up instruments, falling in pits, and picking up keys. The global framerule can really affect your time and there’s nothing you can do about it.

You can put down 2 bombs on a screen transition, or quickly drop 2 in a pit, and any available cutscene will get activated. This is utilized a lot throughout the run. Super jumps

Super jumps are jumps in mid-air. You generally have be clipped into a wall to do them. You also can’t do them if you’re too close to an outwards pointing corner. There are some exceptions to needing to be clipped, but they are irrelevant for now. You can do super jumps using sword, bow, firerod, or without anything equipped. Super jumps without an item are referred to as naked super jumps.

  1. Clip into the wall
  2. Face the wall
  3. Keep pushing into the wall
  4. Jump
  5. Stop pushing into the wall and face away from it  (hold the input used to face away from it)
  6. Use sword/bow/firerod
  7. Super jump!

For naked super jumps it’s slightly different. You have to be facing away from the wall at all times. You can move left/right and keep facing up/down buy jumping and moving. This is also how to transition while facing away from the wall: jump, start holding down + left, and let go during the transition if you don’t have to move anymore afterwards.

Nightmare Fight

When the transition into this room is going on, mash bombs as fast as you can and keep doing this until you land. If you did it correctly, you’ll skip some text and save 10 seconds. Equip sword and boots. This boss fight has 6 phases:

  1. Blob: There’s a quick kill for the blob, but it’s partially RNG based. You need to stand underneath him, on a specific vertical pixel. You can very easily line up for this by bonking against the top wall. Don’t hold up when you dash, because you might land closer to the wall than you want to. After you line up, hold your sword up. Make sure your sword is somewhere close to being in between the blob’s eyes. Let go of your spin slash (or just stop holding your sword if it isn’t charged) and wait for him to jump. Start holding your sword out again and he’ll be stunlocked. You’ll hear him jump at least once. If he jumps a second time, you got the quick kill. If he goes away, you didn’t get the quick kill. This is RNG based. If you didn’t get it, equip boots and powder. Sprinkle powder on him when he jumps, and do it again when he’s about to disappear. Mashing powder works best for this. You have to hit him 3 times with powder if you missed the text skip and 2 times if you missed the quick kill. If you missed the text skip the blob will be in a different position and you’ll have to stand somewhere else to get the quick kill. Line up the top of Link’s head with the top of the whale on the floor while facing left. You can also be a pixel higher.                                                                                                                                                                                                   You can charge your sword to the left to make it easier to line up. Horizontal position doesn’t matter much, just make sure you’re somewhere below the blob. When you get into position, let go of your spin slash (or just stop holding your sword if it isn’t charged) and wait for him to jump. Start holding your sword out again and he’ll be stunlocked.
  2. Agahnim: Agahnim is going to shoot balls at Link. The first two are guaranteed energy balls, after that it’s a 50% chance for a blue ball. Blue balls are bad because you can’t deflect them. Charge your sword facing away from agahnim. Deflect all of his projectiles until you hit him 4 times total. He’ll move on to the next phase after 4 hits. You can kind of manipulate agahnim: If he moves back to the same place, he’ll give you the same type of ball if you stay on the same horizontal pixel. You can move an odd amount of pixels to get the other kind of ball. Lining up with him in the exact middle makes this easier because he won’t move you horizontally. For example, if you get a blue ball and he moves to the same place again, move 1 pixel to the right and he’ll give you an energy ball.
  3. Moldorm: There’s a quick kill for moldorm. Stand around here:                                                                                                     Charge your sword up. Release your spin slash when the black circle starts getting bigger. Keep slashing your sword quickly and you’ll stunlock him. Hit him a total of 14 times and stop slashing your sword. He won’t move on to the next phase if you don’t stop slashing your sword. In fact, if you hit him 256 times he won’t move on at all and you’ll softlock. If you can’t manage to get the quick kill like this, you can use shovel strats. Dash into the position shown in the image above and equip shovel and sword. Buffer shovel out of the menu and you’ll be dashing in place with your sword out. You only have to hit him 8 times if you use shovel strats. If you miss the quick kill, equip sword and feather and kill him like you killed the regular moldorm in D1.
  4. Ganon: Move into the top part of the top left corner and dash into Ganon 6 times. Try to stay in the corner. Stand underneath him when he’s dying.
  5. Lanmola: Charge your sword down after you kill Ganon and release your spin slash when the black circles around lanmola disappear. Dash into him if you miss.
  6. DethI: Stand below him. Equip boots and boomerang. Face up and shoot the boomerang at his eye when it opens up. You’ll only have to hit him once. The hitbox is a little finicky though. Sometimes it doesn’t work for some reason. When he opens his eye is based on RNG.

After you kill DethI, move into the left part of the bottom left corner. Some text boxes will pop up. Start holding up + right when they do and close the text boxes.
